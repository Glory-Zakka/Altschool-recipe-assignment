Nigerian Jollof Rice

This classic West African dish is a flavour-packed one-pot rice cooked in a rich tomato-pepper base. It’s perfect for parties and family dinners. Serve with fried plantain and grilled chicken for the full experience. Don't forget the smoky “party jollof” vibe!

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes
Cook time: 45–55 minutes
Servings: 6
Difficulty: Beginner–Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice (rinsed)
  • 1 large onion (sliced; reserve some for the tomato base)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 3–4 ripe tomatoes (or 400g canned tomatoes)
  • 2 red bell peppers (tatashe), deseeded
  • 1–2 Scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo), to taste
  • 2–3 tbsp tomato paste (for deeper colour)
  • 2–3 cups chicken or vegetable stock (as needed)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika (optional, for “party” flavour)
  • Salt and seasoning cubes/powder to taste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (optional: peas, carrots)
  • 2 tbsp butter (optional, for finish)

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, Scotch bonnet, and part of the onions until smooth; set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat; sauté remaining sliced onions until translucent.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 2–3 minutes to remove raw taste.
  4. Add the blended pepper mix; cook down, stirring, until the sauce reduces and oil begins to separate.
  5. Season with thyme, curry powder, paprika, bay leaves, salt, and seasoning; pour in stock and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add rinsed rice; stir to combine. Reduce heat to low–medium, cover tightly, and cook until rice is almost tender.
  7. Stir gently (avoid mush), add mixed vegetables if using, and adjust with a splash of stock or water if needed.
  8. Cover with foil and lid for a final steam on low heat until rice is fluffy and smoky; finish with butter if desired.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning, then serve hot with protein and fried plantain.

Tips & Notes

For the signature “party” flavour, allow slight caramelisation at the bottom on low heat (not burning). Use a wider pot for even heat. Rinse rice thoroughly to reduce starch and clumping. If using very hot Scotch bonnet, start small; you can always add more.
Serving idea: Pair with grilled chicken, suya, coleslaw, and fried plantain.
